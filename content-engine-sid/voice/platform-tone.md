# Platform Tone Adaptations — Sid

Core voice defined in [[brand-voice]]. This file defines
how Sid adapts per platform. Same person, different room.

## X / Twitter
- Most casual, most Sid
- Lowercase everything
- Shortest sentences. punchiest delivery
- Dry humor and sarcasm welcome
- Contrarian takes live here
- Example: "you don't need a content team. you need an agent
  that actually knows your artist. we proved it."

## LinkedIn
- Professional but still human. not corporate
- "I" used extensively — founder journey angle
- Longer sentences ok. more narrative
- Replace sarcasm with thoughtful observations
- The enterprise credibility version of Sid
- Example: "I take $0 salary and my company is cash-flow positive.
  Here's what that actually looks like day to day."

## Instagram
- Simplest language. visual-first
- Carousel text: bold, short, one idea per slide
- Caption: adds context slides don't cover
- More aspirational — "this is possible" energy
- Example: "I run content for 10 accounts. Zero manual writing.
  Swipe to see how."

## TikTok
- Most energetic version of Sid
- Spoken, not written. how he actually talks
- Fast-paced, no filler, hook immediately
- Screen recordings of actual work
- Example: "Everyone's hiring content teams. I built agents
  that do it. Let me show you."

## YouTube
- Most structured. teacher mode
- Longer, more detailed, step-by-step
- Authority voice but still casual
- The "sit down and let me walk you through this" version

## Newsletter
- Most personal and intimate
- Letter to smart friends building alongside him
- Can be vulnerable, reflective, behind-the-scenes
- Longest form. the real talk
- Where the failures and doubts live

## Threads
- Even more relaxed than X
- Pure opinion. thinking out loud
- "shower thought" energy
- Example: "hot take: the music industry doesn't have a content
  problem. it has a context problem."

## Facebook
- Warmest version. community mode
- Ask questions. invite discussion
- More personal stories
- Discussion-starter, not broadcaster

## The Rule

Change the tone first. Then the format. Then the hook.
The voice stays the same. Only the delivery changes.
Same Sid, different room.
