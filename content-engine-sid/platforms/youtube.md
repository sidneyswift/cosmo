# YouTube

## Platform DNA
- Long-form video. sweet spot 8-12 minutes
- Vibe: educational, structured, SEO-driven. YouTube is a
  search engine — people come looking for answers
- Audience: mix of [[builders]] and [[industry]], investing 8+
  minutes so they're more committed than scrollers
- Evergreen content lives here. a good video gets views for years

## Content Rules
- TITLE IS EVERYTHING. SEO-optimized, curiosity-driven, specific.
  "How I Run a Record Label with AI Agents (Full System)" not
  "My AI Setup"
- Thumbnail: bold text, Sid's face (personal brand), contrasting colors
- Match [[brand-voice]] but more structured. see [[platform-tone]]
- Use [[hooks]] in the first 30 seconds — spoken version of best hook
- Structure with clear sections and chapters

## Video Structure
[HOOK — 0:00-0:30]
State the problem + promise the solution. "I run 10 social
accounts for music clients and don't write a single post
manually. By the end of this video you'll know exactly
how the system works."

[CONTEXT — 0:30-2:00]
Why this matters. What most people get wrong about AI + music.

[MAIN CONTENT — 2:00-9:00]
Step-by-step walkthrough. Screen recordings, demos, examples.
3-5 clear sections with timestamps.

[RECAP + CTA — 9:00-10:00]
Summarize. Subscribe or grab the resource.

## SEO Rules
- Title: primary keyword, natural phrasing, under 60 chars
- Description: first 2 lines visible before "show more" —
  put hook and key links there. full description 200-500 words
- Tags: 5-10 relevant, mix broad and specific
- Chapters: timestamps in description for each section

## Posting Strategy
- Frequency: 1-2x/week
- Consistency > frequency on YouTube
- Best days: Tuesday, Thursday, Saturday
- See [[scheduling]]

## Recoupable-Specific Angles
- Full system walkthroughs (the content engine, the agent stack)
- "How I built [specific thing]" tutorials
- Music industry + AI deep dives
- Founder vlogs: building Recoupable, the label, the grind
- Live streams: building in public, coding sessions
