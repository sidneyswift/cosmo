# Audience: Builders

## Who They Are
Indie hackers, solo founders, AI engineers, SaaS builders,
music tech developers, agency owners going AI-native.
Already building something or actively planning to.
Technical enough to implement what you teach.
Revenue range $0-$50k/mo, aspiring to scale with agents.

## What They Want
- Actionable playbooks, not theory. steps they can follow TODAY
- Real numbers. revenue, metrics, costs, time saved
- Tool recommendations with context — not just "use this tool"
  but "here's when and why and what it replaced"
- Systems thinking — how pieces connect, not just individual tactics
- Proof that this actually works at scale, not just demos

## How to Talk to Them
- Direct and specific. "Here's 5 steps" not "consider exploring"
- Show your work. real screenshots, real numbers, real process
- Peer energy — building alongside them, not above them
- Challenge them: "if you're not automating this, you're burning
  time you don't have"
- They respect the $0 salary, self-funded angle — that's credibility

## What Resonates
- Building in public content (real numbers, real failures)
- System architecture breakdowns
- Agent workflow demos with real output
- "How I replaced X with Y" proof posts
- The thesis: same loop for code works for music (and anything)

## What Turns Them Off
- Hype without substance
- "AI will change everything" without showing HOW
- Overpolished, corporate-feeling content
- Vague advice that could apply to any business
