# Audience: Industry

## Who They Are
Music industry professionals — label execs, A&R, managers,
marketing directors, catalog acquirers, distributors.
Not deeply technical. They know the music business inside out
but are uncertain about AI. They've seen the hype, they're
skeptical, but they know something is shifting.

Some have budgets and decision-making power. These are the
people who sign enterprise deals.

## What They Want
- ROI and cost reduction. "This replaced a $5k/mo agency"
  speaks their language
- Results they can show their boss or their board
- Simplified explanations of how it works without needing
  to understand the tech
- Proof that this actually works with real artists, real catalogs
- Case studies from peers (other labels, other managers)

## How to Talk to Them
- Lead with business impact, not technology
- "We reduced content production costs by 80%" not
  "We built an agentic RAG pipeline with artist context"
- Respect their expertise — they know music, we know AI.
  the partnership is the value
- Don't talk down about "traditional" ways of working.
  acknowledge their process, show how AI enhances it
- Enterprise language without corporate cringe: "co-creation",
  "infrastructure", "your team + our agents"

## What Resonates
- Cost savings and efficiency gains (with real numbers)
- Before/after comparisons they can relate to
- The Gatsby Grace story — AI artist that passed the ear test
- The fact that Recoupable runs its own label (skin in the game)
- "We don't just sell tools. We use them ourselves."

## What Turns Them Off
- "AI will replace the music industry" (threatening)
- Pure tech content with no business context
- Dismissing human creativity or expertise
- Overpromising ("fully autonomous label" when we're not there)
- Any whiff of "we don't need music people anymore"
