## Thumbnail Design System Overhaul — v14 → v15 (2:05 AM - 3:04 AM)

### Context
Sid asked to iterate on the thumbnail design template. Ran a draft through the v14 style guide (gpt-image-2 via Vercel AI Gateway), then iterated based on his feedback.

### Evolution
1. **v14 draft** — GUI container framing, pixel typography, Ghibli marketplace scene. Sid's verdict: needs to be "more professional, expensive, and scrollstopping."
2. **Pebble inspiration** — Sid shared 3 Pebble brand thumbnails (jaguar, plane, whale). Key insight: brand name is MASSIVE spanning bottom 30-40% of frame, illustration interacts with letterforms, no article title on image, full-bleed.
3. **v15 draft** — Applied Pebble treatment to Recoup. Full-bleed marketplace + massive RECOUP wordmark. Better but still too busy (cluttered bazaar, competing text labels like "MOSS", "CYCLEPARTS").
4. **Identity question** — Sid asked "is the setting reinforcing the feeling or identity?" Answer: NO. A crowded anime marketplace says craft/indie/local, not AI infrastructure/market leader. The marketplace was a leftover from market.jpeg reference.
5. **v15.1 — Three variants generated:**
   - **A — Signal Tower:** Lone massive tower on open horizon, cables radiating out. Photorealistic feel, dramatic scale.
   - **B — Orbital Network:** Aerial Ghibli-style view of glowing nodes connected across landscape. Most anime/painterly of the three.
   - **C — Monolith:** Enormous memory archive structure in desert, tiny robot at base, vines growing on surface. Silent authority. Very strong.

### Design Principles Established (v15.1)
- Full-bleed, no GUI containers or borders
- One powerful subject against open atmospheric space (60-70% sky/atmosphere)
- RECOUP wordmark massive in bottom 30%, spanning full width
- Zero text besides RECOUP — article title goes in post copy only
- Scene elements interact with letterforms (cables over letters, robot in O, etc.)
- Settings must reinforce Recoup's identity: infrastructure, scale, calm authority
- Avoid: clutter, competing text, literal exposition, crowded scenes, cartoony tone

- `/tmp/thumbnail-v15-test/v15-tower.png`, `v15-orbital.png`, `v15-monolith.png` — the three variants

- Fix: use `http.client.HTTPSConnection` with 180s timeout — works reliably

## Depth-Composite Thumbnail Pipeline (8:38 AM)

### What Sid Asked
Sid wants the Pebble-style *masking* effect where foreground scene elements overlap ON TOP of the wordmark, creating layered depth. Shared two Pebble examples: plane flying in front of "PEBBLE", jaguar standing in front of "PEBBLE".

### What I Built
- **`scripts/depth-thumbnail.py`** — Python compositor that:
  1. Takes any scene image as input
  2. Uses `rembg` to extract the foreground subject
  3. Renders the Recoup SVG wordmark (`text-darkmode.svg`) via `rsvg-convert`
  4. Composites: background scene → wordmark → foreground subject on top
  5. Also adds a ground-plane gradient mask so terrain/grass blends over the wordmark naturally
- Parameters: `--wordmark-y` (vertical position), `--wordmark-scale` (size), `--wordmark-svg`
- Also created `scripts/test-depth-thumbnail.js` for end-to-end testing with gpt-image-2

### Test Results
- Generated a Ghibli-style robot-on-hilltop scene via gpt-image-2
- rembg cleanly extracted the robot subject
- v1 composite: robot overlaps wordmark ✅ but grass/terrain didn't mask
- v2 composite: added ground-plane gradient blending — grass and terrain now also sit in front of the wordmark ✅
- Output posted to #cosmo-content thread

### Technical Details
- SVG rendering: `rsvg-convert` (from homebrew librsvg) — clean white-on-transparent
- PNG fallback: strips black pixels from `text-darkmode.png` to make transparent (works but lower quality)
- rembg model: u2net, downloads ~176MB on first run
- Intermediate files saved to `/tmp/depth-thumbnail/` for debugging
- The compositing pipeline adds ~10-15 seconds to thumbnail generation

**Late Night Breakthrough — Pebble Masking Finally Understood (11:48 PM):**
- My earlier approach was WRONG: I was keeping subjects AWAY from the wordmark to keep it readable
- Pebble's approach: subjects actively CROSS THROUGH the text. The depth IS the overlap.
- The plane slices through "PEBBLE", the jaguar's body overlaps the letters — but the text stays readable because the subject is a clean silhouette with contrast
- The headphones thumbnail nailed this: headband arches IN FRONT of "Recoup", figure sits on the band, ear cups frame the scene. Every letter readable.
- Sid called me out for not checking my work — "ALWAYS CHECK YOUR WORK!" noted in MEMORY.md
- Key rule: subjects must be clean silhouettes that cross 1-2 letters max, not 3+
