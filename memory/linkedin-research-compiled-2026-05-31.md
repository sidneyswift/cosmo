## TOF (Top of Funnel) — What Gets Reach in 2026

### Algorithm Changes
- Organic reach down 50% YoY, follower growth down 59%
- March 2026 "Authenticity Update" — NLP detects and shadowbans engagement bait ("Comment YES for the PDF" is dead)
- New primary ranking signal: **Depth Score** (dwell time replaced likes as #1)
- 3-phase evaluation: 2-5% of network sees post first → engagement velocity in first 60-90 minutes determines everything → phase 3 = 40-60x more impressions
- **External links get 60% reach penalty** — zero-click content only
- Interest-based distribution replacing connection-based — LinkedIn surfaces content to non-followers who match topical interest
- "...more" clicks now a positive signal (curiosity hooks work)
- Posting frequency: 2-3x/week optimal. Daily posting = diminishing returns. 1 exceptional/week > 5 average

### Format Rankings (Current 2026 Performance)
1. **Multi-image posts (3-4 personal photos)** — 6.60% engagement rate. Best overall. Personal > stock.
2. **Carousels / PDF documents** — 596% more engagement than text-only, 4x more reach than last year. Sweet spot: 6-10 slides, <12 words/slide, hook on slide 1.
3. **Polls** — 206% more reach. Not anonymous — can see who voted (prospecting gold).
4. **Text-only** — Still works with strong writing. No image > bad image.
5. **Video** — Reach crashed 35%, but builds trust. 30-90 sec, always captions (85% watch silent), native upload only.
6. **Single images** — DEAD. 30% worse than plain text. The 2024 "always add an image" advice is actively hurting reach.

### TOF Content Types That Work
- "How I [did X]" outperforms "How to [do X]" — readers want stories, not advice
- Failure stories often outperform wins — authentic vulnerability creates connection
- Unpopular opinions backed by evidence
- Perspective shifts — "what I used to believe vs what I know now"
- Behind-the-scenes — real process, not polished version
- Recommended mix: 20% TOF (contrarian takes, bold predictions), 60% MOF (frameworks, guides), 20% BOF (client stories)

### Anti-Patterns (Penalized in 2026)
- Engagement bait — shadowbanned by NLP detection
- Topic-hopping — algorithm rewards topical consistency, punishes random pivoting
- Generic "thoughts?" endings
- Corporate/polished tone — reads as AI-generated
- Tagging people for reach (without real reason)
- Posting company page links
- "Broetry" (one-sentence paragraphs with no substance)

## MOF (Middle of Funnel) — What Builds Trust & Pipeline

### What MOF Content Does
- Moves people from "I know this person" to "I trust this person's judgment"
- 60% of content should be MOF (per 1.8M post analysis)
- MOF = frameworks, how-tos, process breakdowns, lessons, "how we actually did it"

### MOF Content Types That Work
1. **"Here's exactly how we did it"** — step-by-step process posts showing real implementation
2. **Framework posts** — original frameworks that name a concept ("The 3-Layer Content Stack")
3. **Before/after breakdowns** — with specific numbers, not vague improvements
4. **Lessons from mistakes** — what went wrong and what you'd do differently
5. **Industry analysis with original data** — not rehashing what everyone else says
6. **Behind-the-curtain decision posts** — "here's why we chose X over Y"
7. **Carousel tutorials** — teaching something specific in 6-10 slides

### Founder-Specific MOF Strategies
- Share the *thinking* behind decisions, not just the decisions
- Write about problems you're actively solving (not solved 5 years ago)
- Build "intellectual property" — named frameworks, models, approaches
- Authority = demonstrated competence, not credentials
- One topic cluster > many random topics. Be the person for ONE thing.

### MOF → Pipeline Connection
- Prospects read 13+ pieces of content before engaging with sales (Gartner)
- MOF content is what they read during those 13 touches
- The "pre-sold demo" — by the time they book, they already believe your approach works
- Key: content should make prospects feel smarter, not sold to

## BOF (Bottom of Funnel) — What Converts

### Hard Numbers
- Wynter: 80% of demo signups from founder-led LinkedIn content
- Aligned: 65% of total leads from founder thought leadership
- RB2B (Adam Robinson): $0→$5M ARR in 12 months, LinkedIn primary channel. 75% of time on content, doesn't write about product
- ClickUp: $300M ARR by systematizing leadership team's social presence
- Drift: $1M→$10M leveraging founder David Cancel's social profile
- Hootsuite: $15M pipeline influenced by CEO's LinkedIn activity

### BOF Content Types (Ranked)
1. **Comparison/alternatives** — 8.43% conversion rate (2x broader keywords). Geekbot BOFU = 2,400% better than TOFU.
2. **Case studies with hard metrics** — lead with number in first sentence. Before/after, team size, onboarding time, what almost went wrong.
3. **ROI calculators** — give CFO a number to approve budget
4. **Transparent pricing** — "Contact Sales" filters OUT buyers
5. **Internal champion content** — decks/one-pagers for the buyer selling to their committee (6-10 person buying committee in B2B)
6. **Objection-handling content** — pre-empt sales call blockers

### How Founders Do BOF Without Being Salesy
- Write about genuine struggles, not product features
- The "vulnerability + authority" mix — heavy on insights and lessons, light on product
- One exceptional post/week > five average posts
- AI saturation makes authenticity MORE valuable — stories AI can't replicate = moat
- Trust is paid in content, collected in demos — 81% of buyers choose vendor before speaking to sales

### CTAs That Work
- Embedded scheduling lifts booking from ~30% to 66.7% (Chili Piper, 4M form submissions)
- Soft CTAs — content does the selling, CTA opens the door. "Here's what we learned" → engagement → DM → demo
- Boost organic winners as Thought Leader Ads (1.7x higher CTR than brand graphics)
- Don't boost mediocre posts — amplify what already works

### Structural Insight
- Only 21% of content programs target BOF — 79% sits in TOF/MOF
- A page with 200 visits and 15 demos beats 10,000 visits and zero
- Three BOF assets to build FIRST: competitor comparison, metrics-backed case study, transparent pricing

## Hooks — What Opens Get Clicks

### Algorithm Mechanics for Hooks
- LinkedIn shows 3 lines in preview: ~210 chars desktop, ~140 mobile (57%+ mobile)
- "...more" click is a positive signal — curiosity hooks work
- First 60-90 minutes determine everything — hook quality drives initial engagement velocity

### Hook Rules
- Keep hooks under 8 words. Use numbers.
- Open with the most surprising/controversial/specific thing
- Never start with "I'm excited to share..." or "Great news!" or "Happy to announce..."
- Don't start with a question (overused, algorithm may be downranking)
- Numbers in hooks always outperform no-numbers
- Specificity > cleverness — "$83K in pipeline from one post" > "A game-changing content strategy"

### 7 Hook Categories with Templates

**1. Contrarian / Hot Take**
- "Everyone says [common belief]. They're wrong."
- "[Industry norm] is broken. Here's proof."
- "Stop doing [popular tactic]. It hasn't worked since [year]."
- "The [thing everyone recommends] is actually killing your [metric]."

**2. Specific Result / Data**
- "We [action] and [specific metric happened]."
- "[Specific number] [metric] in [timeframe]. Here's the breakdown."
- "One change to our [process] increased [metric] by [%]."
- "I analyzed [N] [things] and found [surprising pattern]."

**3. Story / Narrative**
- "Last [timeframe], I [did something risky/unusual]."
- "A [prospect/customer] told me something that changed how I think about [topic]."
- "I almost [drastic action]. Here's what happened instead."
- "The worst [professional moment] I've ever had taught me [lesson]."

**4. Behind-the-Scenes / Transparency**
- "Here's what [process] actually looks like inside our company."
- "Nobody talks about the [ugly reality] of [industry/role]."
- "We tried [thing] and it completely failed. Here's why."
- "The real reason we [decision] (it's not what you think)."

**5. Framework / Teachable**
- "The [number]-step [framework name] that [outcome]."
- "After [experience], I built a [framework] for [problem]. Use it."
- "If [condition], here's exactly what to do (step by step)."
- "[Complex thing] explained in [simple format]."

**6. Challenge / Provocation**
- "If you're still [doing X], you're leaving [money/time/results] on the table."
- "Most [role]s get [topic] completely wrong."
- "Your [strategy/process] is probably failing because of [specific reason]."
- "[Trendy thing] is a distraction. Here's what actually matters."

**7. Curiosity Gap**
- "The [thing] that [major company] does differently (and nobody copies)."
- "I spent [time/money] on [thing] so you don't have to."
- "[Famous person/company] taught me [unexpected lesson] about [topic]."
- "There's a reason [surprising fact]. And it changes everything about [topic]."

### Hook Usage by Funnel Stage
- **TOF:** Contrarian, Curiosity Gap, Challenge — maximize reach, stop the scroll
- **MOF:** Framework, Behind-the-Scenes, Story — demonstrate expertise, build trust
- **BOF:** Specific Result, Story (customer stories), Behind-the-Scenes (process) — prove it works

## Key Strategic Insights

1. **The founder IS the funnel.** Personal profiles get 3-5x more reach than company pages. 82% of B2B buyers trust companies more when CEO is active on social.

2. **One topic > many topics.** Topical consistency is rewarded by algorithm AND builds authority. Be the person for ONE thing.

3. **AI-era moat = "Only I can write this."** Every post should pass the test: could this come from anyone, or does it require MY specific experience/perspective/data?

4. **Zero-click or die.** External links get 60% penalty. All value delivered natively. Link in comments barely helps.

5. **Mobile-first formatting.** 57%+ is mobile. Short lines. Generous whitespace. No walls of text.

6. **Engagement velocity > total engagement.** 10 comments in 30 minutes > 50 comments over 3 days.

7. **Trust compounds, cleverness doesn't.** Consistent authentic perspective over months beats viral one-offs.
