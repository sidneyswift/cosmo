# Platform Tone Adaptations — Recoupable

Core voice defined in [[brand-voice]]. How the company
voice adapts per platform.

## X / Twitter
- Concise product updates and proof posts
- More polished than Sid's personal X
- Feature announcements, customer wins, demos
- Can be punchy but not sarcastic
- Example: "Our platform just replaced a $5k/mo content agency
  for a label. 10 accounts. Zero manual posts. Here's how."

## LinkedIn
- Enterprise credibility mode
- Case studies, thought leadership, industry analysis
- Professional tone, longer format
- Where label execs and decision-makers see us
- Example: "Record labels are spending $5-10k/month on content
  production per artist. We built infrastructure that reduces
  that to near-zero. Here's what the data shows."

## Instagram
- Visual product showcase
- System diagrams, before/after, UI screenshots
- Clean design, consistent branding
- Aspirational but grounded in real product

## TikTok
- Product demos, pure and simple
- "Watch this agent do X in 30 seconds"
- Screen recordings of the platform working
- Less personality than Sid's TikTok, more product

## YouTube
- Product tutorials and walkthroughs
- How-to content for artists and label teams
- Customer story interviews (when available)
- The most educational, detailed content

## Newsletter
- Product updates + industry analysis
- Customer spotlights
- Roadmap previews
- More polished than Sid's personal newsletter

## Threads
- Industry observations from a product perspective
- Lighter than LinkedIn, more focused than Sid's Threads
- Product philosophy, not personal opinions

## Facebook
- Community engagement around music + AI
- Discussion prompts about industry trends
- Product announcements for broader audience

## The Rule

Recoupable always sounds like a company you'd trust with your
catalog and your budget. Confident, capable, proven.
Never edgy, never risky, never personal.
