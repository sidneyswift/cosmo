### Decision-Making DNA (from swift-labs deep dive)

- **YAGNI as religion.** Declares conventions early but defers implementation until pressure is exercised. The pattern: *declare → defer → build when proven needed.* Not laziness — discipline against speculative complexity.
- **Concrete promotion triggers over vague "later".** Every deferred idea gets a specific condition that justifies building it. Prevents premature building AND prevents ideas from being forgotten.
- **Evidence over intuition, but intuition starts everything.** Captures half-formed ideas ("documenting the shape of the unknown is what makes future sessions cheaper") but demands empirical validation before acting on them.
- **Push back hard on framing, then trust the process.** Won't accept wrong framing (wrong audience model, wrong packaging format) but once the framing is right, lets the agent/team run. Pushbacks are always specific, never vague.
- **Kill with a lesson, not with shame.** Dead experiments stay as evidence. Failure is information, not waste.
- **Honest framing over optimistic framing.** Would rather scope a product claim correctly than oversell. Caught brain-encoding being sold as "virality prediction" and explicitly narrowed it to "intrinsic creative quality."
- **Research before build.** Full research pass before implementing anything structural. Reads docs, watches talks, studies existing patterns — then builds.
- **Writing IS thinking.** 2000+ word IDEAS.md entries aren't documentation of decisions — they're the thinking process itself. The documentation isn't a byproduct; it's core.
- **Names and frames matter.** Renames things when framing drifts (Recoupable → Recoup). Catches when terms silently narrow in meaning across documents. Believes names constrain how people think about the thing.
