# GTM Asset Types

Six use cases for HTML lead magnets. Each includes structure guidance and what to include.

## 1. Lead Magnets (Cold Outbound)

**Purpose:** Demonstrate expertise before a conversation. Not a generic PDF — an interactive research resource prospects bookmark.

**Structure:**
- Sticky nav with 4-6 sections
- Industry-specific data tables and benchmarks
- Interactive readiness checklist or scoring rubric
- Embedded framework or methodology breakdown
- Offer/CTA section at the bottom of every page/tab

**Include:** Real buyer data, industry benchmarks, actionable checklists, 3-5 navigable sections. Prospect should spend 10-15 minutes exploring.

## 2. Custom Proposals

**Purpose:** Replace the 12-page PDF proposal. Tabbed, navigable, scoped to the prospect's business.

**Structure:**
- Tabs: Situation Analysis | Approach | Timeline | Pricing | FAQ
- Interactive pricing (toggle options, see totals update)
- Visual week-by-week workflow
- Prospect-specific metrics and language
- Objection-handling FAQ

**Include:** Their industry, their metrics, their language. Toggle pricing options. Visual timeline. Address specific objections before they're voiced.

## 3. Pre-Demo Deliverables

**Purpose:** Send after a positive reply, before the first call. Turn a first meeting into a second meeting.

**Structure:**
- Custom audit or analysis (3-5 sections)
- Scoring or grading rubric with their data
- Competitive comparison table
- Recommendations section
- "Next steps" CTA

**Include:** Data from their website, LinkedIn, public filings. Custom analysis that could only come from researching them specifically. The prospect arrives already having experienced what you do.

## 4. Sales Pages

**Purpose:** Higher-converting alternative to a capability deck. Prospect explores at their own pace.

**Structure:**
- Hero with value prop
- Navigable sections by prospect concern
- Industry benchmarks (theirs, not generic)
- Process visualization
- Social proof / case studies
- "Copy as brief" export button

**Include:** Relevant testimonials, process diagrams, interactive elements. Make it feel like the agency is already doing the work.

## 5. Client Onboarding Docs

**Purpose:** Post-close delivery format for operationally complex material. Replace the 50-page PDF nobody reads.

**Structure:**
- Table of contents with jump links
- Phase/week tabs
- Collapsible detail sections (accordions)
- Reference tables (contacts, logins, schedules)
- Checklist sections with progress tracking

**Include:** Campaign strategy, targeting frameworks, SOPs, launch roadmaps. Client can access exactly the section they need.

## 6. Intelligence Briefs

**Purpose:** Internal research assets for campaign planning, market entry, or offer development.

**Structure:**
- Tabbed by research domain (competitors, ICP, market, angles)
- Data tables with sortable columns
- Annotated frameworks and diagrams
- Key findings summary at top
- Searchable/filterable content

**Include:** CRM data analysis, competitive intel, buyer psychology, market sizing. Organized, tabbed, searchable — not a 200-line doc nobody reads past line 50.
